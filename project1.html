Final Year Project

Investigation of Heat Generation During Bobbin Tool Friction Stir Welding on Dissimilar Aluminum Alloy

Abstract

This project focuses on the experimental investigation of heat generation during Bobbin Tool Friction Stir Welding (BT-FSW) of dissimilar aluminum alloys. The objective was to understand the influence of process parameters such as tool rotation speed and welding speed on heat generation, material flow, and joint quality.

Methodology

The work involved conducting welding experiments with different sets of process parameters. Heat generation was measured using temperature sensors, while weld quality was examined through metallurgical analysis and tensile strength testing. Data was compared across samples to establish the effect of parameter variation.

Results & Conclusion

The study showed that higher tool rotational speeds increased heat input, improving material mixing but also risking excess softening. Optimized process conditions led to defect-free welds with acceptable mechanical properties. The project demonstrated that Bobbin Tool FSW is effective for welding dissimilar aluminum alloys with reduced distortion compared to conventional methods.
